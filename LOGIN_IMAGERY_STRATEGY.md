# samm Login Imagery Strategy

## Purpose
This document locks the visual strategy for imagery on the `samm` login screen so implementation can proceed without guesswork.

## Final Decision
Use a `2x2 image grid` on the brand panel of the login screen.

Do not use a slideshow unless the grid fails visually during implementation.

## Why This Direction
The 2x2 grid is the correct default because it:
- communicates audience breadth immediately
- feels editorial and intentional
- avoids the weakness of generic hero carousels
- supports multiple customer archetypes without adding interaction overhead
- gives the login screen emotional context without sacrificing clarity

## Objective
The imagery should make the right customer feel:
- this is built for people like me
- this understands the pressure of running marketing and operations
- this will help me reduce chaos and regain control

The emotional promise is:
- less marketing chaos
- more coordinated execution
- calmer operational control

## Audience To Depict
The imagery should reflect the people `samm` is for:
- founders
- operators
- small business owners
- lean marketing teams
- coordinators and managers working across multiple channels

These should not look like abstract enterprise personas. They should look like real working people with responsibility.

## Visual Tone
The images should feel:
- grounded
- calm
- capable
- modern
- lightly cinematic
- business-relevant
- human

They should not feel:
- corporate stock-photo polished
- staged or generic
- chaotic or frantic
- overly youthful startup hype
- luxury-lifestyle aspirational
- fake teamwork theater

## Cultural / Market Fit
Whenever possible, the imagery should feel globally relevant and especially comfortable for African and emerging-market business contexts without becoming regionally narrow or performative.

That means:
- real workplaces
- practical devices
- modest but professional environments
- modern clothing without corporate costume energy
- people who look like decision-makers and operators, not ad models

## Grid Composition
Use four windows in a 2x2 arrangement.

Each image should feel related in tone but distinct in subject.

Recommended panel roles:
1. Founder or owner reviewing performance calmly on a laptop
2. Small team planning or aligning around work
3. Operator approving or monitoring from a dashboard
4. Business owner or coordinator in a composed working moment, relieved rather than overwhelmed

## Image Framing
Prefer:
- medium and medium-wide shots
- clear facial expression without exaggerated smiling
- visible devices, notebooks, dashboards, or work context
- natural light or soft controlled light
- subtle depth and texture

Avoid:
- extreme closeups
- empty office interiors with no people
- handshakes
- stock meeting-room clichés
- chaotic desk messes
- obviously fake staged pointing at screens

## Login Panel Role
The image grid belongs on the brand side of the split login layout.

Its role is to provide:
- audience relevance
- emotional reassurance
- visual warmth
- product context beyond pure authentication

It should support the wordmark and short promise, not overpower the form.

## Supporting Copy Goals
The copy near the imagery should say, in effect:
- this is for business people carrying too much coordination overhead
- `samm` helps absorb that pressure
- the system brings order to marketing and execution work

Preferred message directions:
- less marketing chaos
- work that stays coordinated
- control without constant firefighting
- one system keeping the flow aligned

## Recommended Copy Routes
Option A:
- `samm`
- `less marketing chaos`
- `keep the work moving without carrying every task yourself`

Option B:
- `samm`
- `where agents align`
- `coordinate campaigns, approvals, and execution from one calm control layer`

Option C:
- `samm`
- `orchestrate the work`
- `for operators, founders, and teams who need marketing to move without the noise`

## Styling Rules For The Grid
- moderate border radius
- no heavy shadows
- no thick white frames around each image
- maintain tonal consistency across all four images
- preserve enough contrast against the surrounding panel
- do not make the grid feel like a social feed or media gallery

## Interaction Rules
Default behavior:
- static image grid

Optional enhancement later:
- very subtle fade-in on page load
- slight hover polish only if it remains quiet

Do not add:
- auto-rotation
- sliding animation
- carousel controls
- attention-seeking motion

## Implementation Guidance
When implemented in the login screen:
- keep the split layout
- place the 2x2 grid in the brand panel
- pair it with short reassurance copy and the `samm` wordmark
- ensure the form panel stays simple and frictionless
- let the images humanize the experience, not dominate it

## Success Criteria
The imagery is successful if a first-time visitor immediately understands:
- who this product is for
- what kind of pressure it helps relieve
- that the system is serious, calm, and business-usable

## Summary
The login imagery should humanize `samm` without making it feel like a lifestyle brand. The correct direction is a calm 2x2 grid showing founders, operators, and small teams in real work contexts, supported by concise copy about reducing chaos and keeping execution aligned.
